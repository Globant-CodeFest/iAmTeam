According to the climatic disasters that have occurred, we recommend

  • Disaster resilience

    Sustainable farming practices are designed to promote the resilience of farming systems to natural disasters. For example, soil conservation and water management can help reduce erosion and nutrient loss during floods and droughts, allowing for better crop recovery after the event.

  • Soil conservation

    Developing sustainable agricultural practices, such as mulching and crop rotation, can improve soil structure and health. This can increase its ability to absorb and retain water during heavy rain events, thus reducing the risk of flooding and soil erosion.

  • Water management

    Sustainable agricultural practices promote efficient use of water, which can be crucial during droughts or periods of water scarcity. The use of efficient irrigation techniques and rainwater harvesting can help maintain the availability of water for crops and reduce dependence on external sources.

  • Biodiversity and pest control

    The implementation of agroecological and agroforestry approaches in sustainable agriculture promotes biodiversity in agricultural systems. This can provide a better balance in ecosystems and help control pests and diseases naturally, reducing the need to use chemical pesticides.
